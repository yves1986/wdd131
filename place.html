Côte d'Ivoire

The Pearl of West Africa

About the Country

Côte d'Ivoire, also known as Ivory Coast, is a West African country bordered by Liberia, Guinea, Mali, Burkina Faso, and Ghana. It is the world's largest producer of cocoa beans, contributing to about 40% of the global supply.

The country gained independence from France in 1960 and has since become one of the most economically stable nations in West Africa. The official language is French, and the capital city is Yamoussoukro, while Abidjan serves as the economic capital.

Côte d'Ivoire is known for its vibrant culture, traditional music, and diverse ethnic groups including the Akan, Krou, and Mandé peoples.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Yamoussoukro
  • Population: ~27 million
  • Official Language: French
  • Currency: West African CFA Franc
  • Largest City: Abidjan

Top Attractions

Basilica of Our Lady of Peace

The largest church in the world, located in Yamoussoukro, inspired by St. Peter's Basilica in Rome.

Tai National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the last remaining primary rainforests in West Africa.

Grand-Bassam

A historic colonial town and UNESCO World Heritage site with beautiful beaches and architecture.

Abidjan's St. Paul's Cathedral

A stunning modernist cathedral with a unique architectural design overlooking the city.
